Facility Upgrades Help Existing Properties Support Changing Business Needs

Businesses expand, employee requirements change, tenants rotate, systems age and customer expectations evolve. A property that worked effectively several years ago may no longer support its current layout, occupancy or operational priorities.

RCC-BGM develops upgrade projects around the existing property and the outcome the customer wants to achieve—one high-priority area or several connected improvements through a phased project.

The process begins with current condition, intended future use, operating restrictions and connected HVAC, MEP, construction and finish requirements.

Commercial facility upgrades FAQs

Questions Property Teams Ask

What are commercial facility upgrades?

They are planned improvements to an existing property that improve functionality, usability, presentation or changing operational requirements.

How are upgrades different from maintenance?

Maintenance keeps existing components in service; an upgrade intentionally improves or changes part of the facility.

Can upgrades be completed in an occupied building?

Yes. Projects may be phased around employees, tenants, customers, access, noise and temporary closures.

Can HVAC and MEP work be included?

Approved HVAC, mechanical, electrical and plumbing requirements can be coordinated with the wider facility plan.

Can you modernize an office?

Workplace upgrades may include offices, meeting rooms, collaboration areas, reception, employee spaces and related finishes.

Can you upgrade common areas?

Yes. Lobbies, corridors, entrances, shared restrooms, lounges and selected customer-facing areas can be assessed.

How are facility upgrades priced?

Pricing depends on building condition, scope, materials, equipment, access, phasing, trades and technical requirements.

Is a site assessment required?

A site review is strongly recommended because existing conditions and connected systems affect the final scope.
