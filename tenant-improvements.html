From leased space to functional workplace

A commercial space must support the business that will use it

A space may be available but still unsuitable for employees, customer flow, meeting areas, equipment, storage or daily operations.

Tenant improvements adapt the space around approved business use. RCC-BGM reviews existing conditions, tenant requirements, landlord conditions and connected systems before organizing the project.

Understand the space before committing

Pre-lease and preconstruction review

An early review can identify visible construction categories before the build-out plan or occupancy schedule is finalized.

  • Existing layout and property condition
  • Intended business use and employee capacity
  • HVAC, electrical, plumbing and ceiling conditions
  • Access, deliveries and target move-in date

Important boundary

A construction walkthrough is not a substitute for architectural, engineering, legal, lease or formal code review. Qualified professionals and landlord approval may still be required.

Understanding lease and project budget responsibilities

Tenant improvement allowances are separate from construction scope

A landlord may provide an allowance toward approved construction costs, but it does not automatically define the complete budget or cover every tenant requirement.

Allowance terms, eligible costs and reimbursement should be confirmed by tenant, landlord and their legal or real-estate representatives. RCC-BGM can separately identify the construction scope and pricing basis.

Questions from tenant and property teams

Tenant improvement services FAQs

What are tenant improvements?

Construction and renovation changes inside a commercial property to prepare or modify space for a specific business occupant.

What is a tenant build-out?

Completing or reconfiguring a commercial suite according to approved tenant layout and business requirements.

Can RCC-BGM improve an existing office?

Yes. Office layout changes, interior modifications, painting, carpentry, HVAC and MEP requirements can be assessed.

Can you build out a vacant commercial suite?

Vacant or unfinished spaces may be assessed for a coordinated tenant build-out.

Can you coordinate HVAC and MEP?

HVAC and MEP requirements may be coordinated with the interior construction plan.

Can work happen in an occupied office?

Work may be divided into phases and scheduled around approved hours and access restrictions.

Do you work with landlords and property managers?

Planning can include landlord, tenant and property-management requirements when responsibilities are clearly defined.

Can RCC-BGM help before signing a lease?

RCC-BGM may review visible construction considerations, but legal, lease, design and formal code review remain separate.

Can you guarantee a move-in date?

Completion depends on approvals, materials, trades, inspections, decisions and existing conditions. An unconditional guarantee should not be assumed.
