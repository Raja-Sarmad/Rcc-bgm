Connecting building systems with the physical project

MEP Work Should Not Be Planned Separately From the Building

Mechanical, electrical and plumbing systems affect how a commercial property functions. Changes to one system can create requirements for another.

A new HVAC installation may require electrical power, drainage, controls, ceiling access and physical construction. An office kitchen may require plumbing, electrical connections, ventilation and interior finish work.

RCC-BGM coordinates approved MEP requirements within the wider commercial project so building systems, construction work and facility operations can be reviewed together.

System modifications around active operations

Planning MEP Work in Occupied Buildings

MEP projects may affect temperature, power, water access, noise levels and usable areas. Occupied properties therefore require careful scheduling and communication.

  • Business hours, tenant schedules and customer traffic
  • Mechanical-room, electrical-room and ceiling access
  • Water, power and HVAC shutdown windows
  • Temporary closures, weekend work and security requirements

Work may be phased by floor, department, tenant suite or system, with planned interruptions communicated in advance.

Commercial MEP services FAQs

Questions Commercial Property Teams Ask

What does MEP mean in commercial construction?

MEP refers to mechanical, electrical and plumbing building systems and the coordination required between those systems and the wider construction project.

What are commercial MEP services?

They may include system assessment, requirement identification, trade coordination and integration with construction or facility projects.

Is RCC-BGM an MEP contractor?

The exact role depends on location, licenses and participating qualified trades. It should not be described as universally licensed without verification.

Can MEP services be included in a tenant improvement?

Yes. Tenant build-outs frequently require HVAC, electrical, plumbing and construction coordination.

Can MEP work require power or water shutdowns?

Yes. Temporary interruptions should be planned, approved and communicated before work begins.

Do you provide MEP engineering or drawings?

Engineering and permit drawings should only be offered when qualified professionals and contractual responsibility are confirmed.

Can you coordinate MEP in an occupied office?

Work may be phased around operating hours, access requirements and planned service interruptions.

How is commercial MEP work priced?

Pricing depends on existing conditions, technical scope, equipment, materials, access, shutdowns, permits and construction dependencies.
