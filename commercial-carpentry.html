Commercial Carpentry Should Support Both Function and Finish

Carpentry influences how a commercial interior functions, how occupants move through the building and how completed construction work appears. A damaged door can affect access, worn frames and trim can reduce the presentation of a customer-facing area, and a tenant improvement may require several carpentry components to be coordinated with walls, painting and building systems.

RCC-BGM reviews the existing conditions, measurements, materials, occupancy and connected construction requirements before developing the commercial carpentry scope. The objective is to define the work clearly before materials are ordered or active areas are affected.

Measured Requirements

Review dimensions, materials, hardware, existing damage and surrounding commercial finishes before finalizing the scope.

Connected Planning

Coordinate carpentry with painting, tenant improvements, maintenance construction and approved MEP requirements.

Occupied-Facility Awareness

Plan access, work zones, material handling and service timing around employees, tenants and active commercial operations.

Materials and finish selection

Commercial Carpentry Materials Should Match the Environment

Material selection affects appearance, durability, maintenance and project cost. The appropriate product depends on the component, traffic, moisture exposure, cleaning requirements and surrounding commercial finishes.

Existing Material Review

Review the current profile, dimensions, colour, texture, finish and condition before selecting a replacement.

Use and Durability

Consider traffic, impact, moisture, maintenance and the daily use of the commercial space.

Product Availability

Aged or discontinued materials may require an approved alternative or wider replacement for a more consistent finish.

Important Finish-Matching Expectation

Existing commercial materials may change through age, sunlight, wear and repeated painting. RCC-BGM can review available products and samples, but an exact match should not be assumed where the original product has changed or is no longer manufactured.

Active commercial properties

Carpentry Work Planned Around Occupied Buildings

Offices and commercial buildings may remain active while carpentry repairs or improvements are delivered. Work areas, temporary access restrictions and material handling should be planned around the people using the property.

Larger requirements may be divided room by room, floor by floor or area by area according to the approved facility schedule.

Occupied-Facility Planning May Consider

  • Employee and tenant schedules
  • Customer traffic
  • Noise-sensitive periods
  • Dust and cutting controls
  • Temporary barriers
  • Door and corridor access
  • Material deliveries
  • Furniture protection
  • Security procedures
  • Weekend or after-hours work
  • Emergency exit access

Common questions

Commercial Carpentry Services FAQs

What are commercial carpentry services?
Commercial carpentry services include approved repair, installation and finish work for doors, frames, trim, interior components and business-facility improvements.
Does RCC-BGM provide office carpentry services?
RCC-BGM can assess carpentry requirements for offices, reception areas, conference rooms, employee spaces and other approved commercial interiors.
Can carpentry be included in a tenant improvement project?
Yes. Approved carpentry can be coordinated with interior construction, painting, HVAC, MEP and other tenant-improvement requirements.
Do you repair commercial doors and frames?
Commercial interior doors and frames can be assessed for repair, adjustment or replacement according to their condition, material, hardware and surrounding construction.
Do you install commercial interior trim?
Approved interior trim installation, replacement and repair may be included within a commercial carpentry scope.
Can RCC-BGM build custom reception counters?
Selected reception-counter or interior woodwork requirements can be assessed. Fully custom fabrication and architectural millwork require confirmation of the design, fabrication and installation responsibilities.
Can you match existing commercial trim and finishes?
Existing materials and finishes can be reviewed, but an exact match may not be possible when products are aged, altered or discontinued.
Can commercial carpentry be completed in an occupied building?
Work may be phased around employees, tenants, customers and approved building-access periods.
Do you coordinate painting after carpentry?
Commercial painting and finish work may be coordinated when included in the approved project scope.
Can carpentry be coordinated with electrical or plumbing work?
Yes. Carpentry can be planned around approved mechanical, electrical and plumbing requirements. Licensed technical work remains the responsibility of qualified parties.
Does RCC-BGM provide structural carpentry?
Structural carpentry should only be confirmed after the project, licensing, engineering and exact construction responsibilities have been reviewed.
Is a property assessment required?
A property assessment and accurate field measurements are recommended before materials, pricing and the final commercial carpentry scope are confirmed.
How are commercial carpentry services priced?
Pricing depends on measurements, materials, finish requirements, component condition, access, hardware, painting and connected construction work.
What information should we provide?
Provide the property location, facility type, affected doors, frames, trim or other components, approximate quantities, current occupancy, desired finish, photographs and preferred timeline.
