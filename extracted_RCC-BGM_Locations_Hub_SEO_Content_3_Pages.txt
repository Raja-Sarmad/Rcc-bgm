1. Page Role and SEO Intent
The Locations page is a geographic navigation and qualification hub. It should help
a visitor answer: Does RCC-BGM serve my market, and which local page
should I visit? It should not repeat full service-page content or imply that every
RCC and BGM capability is available in every city.
Primary phrases can include commercial facility service locations, commercial
cleaning service areas, commercial construction service areas, facility
maintenance locations and commercial property services near me. The
phrase “near me” should appear sparingly and naturally; city pages should target
the actual city and service intent.

4. What Users Should Find on the Locations Hub
• A search or filter for state, metro area or city
• Clear grouping of current city pages by region
• A short description of RCC and BGM service categories
• A visible statement that services vary by location
• Links to Services, Industries, Why RCC-BGM and Contact
• A location-confirmation CTA for unlisted cities
• No fake office addresses, misleading maps or unsupported local claims

5. Services Available by Market
The hub should present service families rather than claiming universal availability.
Each city page can link to the most relevant dedicated service pages after the local
scope has been verified.

6. How Location Qualification Works
• Location: city, state, property address and nearby service area.
• Facility: property type, size, buildings, occupancy and operating schedule.
• Service: cleaning, maintenance, HVAC, MEP, construction or a connected
requirement.
• Technical responsibility: licensing, engineering, regulated work and specialist
trades.
• Delivery conditions: access, staffing, materials, equipment, travel and schedule.
• Final confirmation: service should be promised only after the market and scope are
reviewed.

18. Schema, Maps and Technical SEO
• Locations hub: WebPage + ItemList + BreadcrumbList.
• City service-area page: WebPage + Service + BreadcrumbList.
• LocalBusiness: use only for a genuine eligible business location with accurate NAP

16. Local SEO Quality Controls
• Do not copy the same 1,500-word page and swap city names
• Do not add fake local addresses, phone numbers or office photos
• Do not embed a map unless it represents a genuine business location or useful
service-area context
• Do not use LocalBusiness schema for a city with no eligible real location
• Do not list every service if only selected BGM or RCC capabilities are available
• Do not force city names into every heading, card and FAQ
• Do use descriptive internal links between city, service, industry and contact pages
• Do maintain one canonical URL for each city

17. Internal Linking Architecture
The Locations hub should link to every active city page. Each city page should link
back to the hub, the most relevant service pages, the Industries hub and Contact.
Service pages may link to a concise list of priority markets, but they should not
include hundreds of repetitive city links.
• Home -> Locations hub
• Locations hub -> regional group -> city page
• City page -> verified service pages
• City page -> relevant industries
• City page -> nearby genuine city pages
• City page -> contact or assessment form
