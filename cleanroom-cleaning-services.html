Controlled environments require a program designed for the space

A cleanroom cannot be treated like a standard office, warehouse or commercial property. Personnel access, cleaning sequence, approved materials, zone transitions and surface handling all affect the controlled environment.

RCC-BGM develops the program around documented facility requirements instead of applying a generic checklist. Assigned areas, procedures, access, frequencies and customer responsibilities should be clear before service begins.

Questions from controlled-facility teams

Cleanroom cleaning services FAQs

What are cleanroom cleaning services?

Specialized programs for controlled environments where particle control, approved procedures, gowning and restricted access may be required.

Does RCC-BGM clean ISO-classified cleanrooms?

RCC-BGM’s current service information identifies ISO-classified cleanroom cleaning. Exact classes and site capability should be confirmed during assessment.

Do you provide ESD floor maintenance?

ESD floor maintenance is identified as a current capability. Products, methods and testing responsibility must be confirmed for each facility.

Can cleaning follow our SOPs?

Yes. The proposed program should be reviewed against customer-approved cleaning, access, gowning and documentation procedures.

Do personnel follow gowning procedures?

Assigned personnel can be oriented to customer-approved procedures. Exact training and certification requirements should be confirmed.

What areas can be included?

Approved floors, walls, doors, glass, gowning areas, airlocks, transitions, fixtures and support areas may be included.

Do you clean production equipment or chemicals?

Production equipment, chemicals, hazardous materials and technical tools are excluded unless separately reviewed and confirmed.

Do you perform particle-count testing?

Testing or environmental-monitoring responsibility must be separately confirmed and is not assumed in standard cleaning.

What information is needed for a proposal?

Provide facility location, environment type, known classification, room sizes, frequency, SOP requirements, access windows and documentation needs.
