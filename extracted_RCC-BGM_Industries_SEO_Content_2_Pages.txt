7. Healthcare and Medical Offices
Medical offices, clinics and healthcare-support properties may require cleaning
and facility support for waiting, administrative, staff and approved patient-facing
areas. Programs should distinguish routine commercial cleaning from regulated
clinical, biohazard or infection-control responsibilities.
• Waiting areas, reception, offices, staff rooms and approved restrooms
• Cleaning schedules organized around patients, staff and operating hours
• Facility maintenance, HVAC and tenant-improvement coordination
• Clear exclusions for sharps, biohazards, medical waste and regulated disinfection

8. Manufacturing and Industrial Facilities
Manufacturing and industrial properties may combine production-support
spaces, warehouses, administrative offices and employee areas. Service plans
must account for shifts, machinery, site safety rules, restricted zones and
customer-defined operational limits.
• Industrial floor, office, restroom and employee-area cleaning
• Facility maintenance and corrective construction around operations
• Commercial HVAC support for approved conditioned areas
• Phased improvements coordinated with site access and production schedules
Boundary: Hazardous production cleaning, confined spaces, machinery cleaning and
chemical handling require separate qualification.

2. How RCC-BGM Adapts Services by Industry

9. Warehouses and Distribution Centers

• Property use: offices, laboratories, warehouses and medical properties have
different daily demands.
• Operating schedule: service windows may depend on shifts, customers, tenants,
production or controlled access.
• Occupancy and traffic: employee count, visitor flow and shared areas influence
cleaning and maintenance scope.
• Technical environment: HVAC, MEP, cleanroom procedures, equipment and
building systems may affect delivery.
• Risk and responsibility: specialist, regulated and licensed work must be clearly
assigned.
• Required outcome: recurring service, corrective maintenance, tenant preparation
or capital improvement.

Warehouses and logistics facilities need cleaning and maintenance programs
that reflect floor area, loading activity, employee spaces, shift schedules and
vehicle movement. RCC-BGM can combine approved warehouse cleaning with
office support, HVAC, repair construction and facility improvements.

coordinated building services. RCC-BGM can develop recurring office cleaning
and janitorial programs while separately assessing HVAC, facility maintenance,
tenant improvements and workplace upgrades.

• Warehouse floors, administrative offices and employee-support areas

• Multi-site standardization with site-specific assessments

4. Multi-Tenant Commercial Properties
Multi-tenant buildings require coordination between ownership, property
management and individual occupants. Programs may combine common-area
cleaning, tenant communication, facility maintenance, HVAC support and

• Suite-specific service requests and landlord-controlled responsibilities
• Move-in, move-out, make-ready and tenant-improvement coordination
• Phased work around occupants, deliveries and shared access routes

14. Service Selection Guidance
• Choose recurring cleaning when the main need is ongoing cleanliness,
restrooms, floors and facility presentation.
• Choose specialized cleaning when customer procedures, controlled
environments or industrial conditions require separate protocols.
• Choose facility maintenance for recurring upkeep, smaller repairs and
coordinated property condition support.
• Choose HVAC or MEP when the requirement affects heating, cooling, ventilation,
power, plumbing or technical building systems.
• Choose construction for tenant build-outs, renovations, construction-level repairs
or planned facility upgrades.
