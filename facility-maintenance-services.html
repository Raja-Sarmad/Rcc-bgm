Addressing needs before they grow

Building a more proactive facility maintenance program

A maintenance plan should not depend entirely on waiting for visible failure. Periodic property reviews can help identify wear, damage and changing requirements earlier.

Planning may consider building condition, previous repairs, high-use areas, seasonal needs, tenant changes, renovations, recurring problem areas and budget priorities. Formal inspection schedules and system-specific contracts require confirmation of the exact program.

Questions from property teams

Facility maintenance services FAQs

What are facility maintenance services?

Services supporting the physical condition, functionality and upkeep of commercial buildings. They can include repairs, HVAC support, painting, carpentry, improvements and maintenance coordination.

Does RCC-BGM provide commercial property maintenance?

Yes. Support can be developed around approved building repairs, upkeep, HVAC requirements and facility improvements.

Can several maintenance trades be coordinated?

Approved HVAC, MEP, carpentry, painting and construction-related work can be organized within a wider facility plan.

Can maintenance work happen in an occupied building?

Work can be planned around operating hours, employee activity and access requirements. The approach depends on the scope.

Do you provide emergency maintenance?

Time-sensitive requests can be reviewed, but availability and response times must be confirmed for the relevant location.

What is the difference between maintenance and facility management?

Maintenance focuses on the physical building and systems. Facility management may include broader operational and administrative responsibilities.

Can maintenance include painting and carpentry?

Yes. These services can be incorporated into approved maintenance and facility-improvement projects.

Is a facility assessment required?

An assessment is recommended because building condition, access and exact scope affect the proposal.

How do we request a proposal?

Provide the property address, building type, known issues, preferred schedule and any supporting photographs or reports.
