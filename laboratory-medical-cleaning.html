Cleaning based on the environment

Laboratories and medical facilities cannot be treated like standard offices

These properties often combine public areas, offices, technical rooms, controlled spaces, employee facilities and restricted work zones in one building.

RCC-BGM separates general commercial areas from controlled, technical or restricted spaces so inappropriate methods are not applied across the entire facility.

One facility, different control levels

Separating general, controlled and restricted cleaning areas

A laboratory or medical property should not use one universal checklist. Areas can be divided according to access, risk and procedural requirements.

01

General areas

Lobbies, reception, offices, corridors, kitchens, restrooms and waiting areas.

02

Procedure-sensitive

Approved lab floors, gowning areas, controlled corridors and examination spaces.

03

Restricted technical

Active research, equipment, chemical storage and technical procedure rooms.

04

Excluded unless confirmed

Hazardous materials, regulated waste, active experiments and technical tools.

  • Documentation, incidents and supervision

Questions from laboratory and medical teams

Laboratory and medical facility cleaning FAQs

What are laboratory cleaning services?

Facility-specific cleaning programs for approved areas within laboratories, research properties and life-science environments.

Does RCC-BGM clean research laboratories?

RCC-BGM may clean approved floors, corridors, support spaces, offices and controlled areas according to the agreed scope.

Do you clean laboratory equipment?

Technical and research equipment is excluded unless the equipment, method and capability are separately reviewed.

Do you provide medical facility cleaning?

Recurring and periodic cleaning may be developed for approved areas within medical offices, clinics and healthcare-support properties.

Can cleaning be scheduled around patient appointments?

Service timing may be coordinated around appointment hours, staff schedules and authorized access windows.

Can you follow laboratory SOPs?

The proposed scope can be reviewed against customer-approved access, cleaning and documentation procedures.

Do you provide cleanroom cleaning?

Yes. Classified controlled environments should be assessed under the dedicated cleanroom cleaning service.

Do you handle medical or hazardous waste?

Medical, biological, chemical and regulated waste are not automatically included and require separate verification.

What information is needed for a quote?

Provide location, environment type, area, frequency, operating schedule, controlled areas, SOP requirements and known exclusions.
